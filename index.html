AI-Powered Regulatory Guidance for Battery Recycling

Simplifying CSRD and CBAM compliance with intelligent, location-specific regulatory assistance

Executive Summary

European sustainability regulations are becoming increasingly complex and far-reaching, posing significant compliance challenges for businesses. The EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) will require nearly 50,000 companies to report detailed environmental and social information, and the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) is introducing new carbon-cost obligations on imports with a definitive regime starting in 2026.

Battery recycling companies, at the forefront of the green transition, face the dual burden of meeting these evolving reporting standards and carbon regulations. Many lack the in-house expertise or resources to navigate hundreds of pages of legislation and guidance, resulting in uncertainty and risk of non-compliance.

LiCycle AI is a chatbot solution designed to address this problem by providing on-demand, location-specific regulatory guidance. It leverages advanced AI and a curated knowledge base of CSRD and CBAM legislation to answer businesses' questions in real time.

Integrated into a website, LiCycle AI acts as a virtual compliance advisor: a user can ask, "What does CSRD require for a mid-sized recycler in Germany?" or "Does CBAM apply if I ship recycled battery materials to the EU?" and receive an instant, accurate answer citing the relevant regulation.

Problem Statement

Complex Regulations

CSRD and CBAM span 20+ documents and over 600 pages of technical requirements, making interpretation difficult for businesses without specialized legal teams.

Lack of Confidence

Only 42% of companies initially subject to CSRD feel "fully confident" in meeting the new sustainability reporting requirements.

Data Quality Issues

83% of businesses lack confidence that their ESG data is robust enough for CSRD's strict demands, despite significant investments in compliance.

Evolving Requirements

Both CSRD and CBAM are being implemented in phases with ongoing updates, making it difficult for companies to stay current with obligations.

Resource Constraints

Battery recyclers typically focus on technology and scaling operations, with limited resources for regulatory compliance departments.

High Cost of Expertise

Current solutions rely on expensive consultants or time-consuming manual research through dense legal texts.
